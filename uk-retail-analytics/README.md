ðŸ” Problem #1: The Profitability Gap (Sales & Pricing)
The Observation
While the Net Revenue (â‚¬24.69M) seems strong, the YoY Growth is down by 23%. My analysis revealed that revenue is highly concentrated in "Homeware" and "DIY," but these categories are also the most sensitive to competitor pricing.

The Data-Driven Insight

The Solution: I implemented a Price vs. Competitor scatter plot to identify "Overpriced" items. I recommended a shift toward "Premium Bundling" in high-rent areas to increase the Average Basket Value.

ðŸ“¦ Problem #2: The Forecast Mismatch (Supply Chain)
The Observation
The dashboard reveals a Forecast Accuracy of 64.06%. An accuracy below 70% in retail leads to massive capital being tied up in "dead stock" or missed opportunities due to stockouts.

The Data-Driven Insight
The Problem: The "Stockout Risk" KPI shows 91 products are currently trending toward zero stock while demand is rising.

The Solution: I created a Heatmap of Stock Coverage.

Instead of just looking at "Units in Hand," I calculated Weeks of Coverage (Current Stock / Predicted Weekly Demand).

This allows the Supply Chain team to see exactly where the North region is overstocked on "Gardening" during winter, allowing for internal stock transfers to the West region, reducing the need for new orders.

ðŸ“Š Key Metrics Developed (DAX)
To go beyond basic sums, I engineered specific business measures:

Forecast Bias: VAR Bias = [Total Units Sold] - [Demand Forecast] -> Identifies if we are consistently over or under-ordering.

Stock Health Score: A 3-level conditional indicator (Critical, Warning, Optimal) based on the ratio of Inventory vs. Demand.

Estimated Margin: Calculated after accounting for discounts and regional rent impact.
